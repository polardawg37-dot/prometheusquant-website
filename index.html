The Engine

MLB In-Game
Win Probability

The core system computes real-time win probabilities for every active MLB game by combining an empirical baseline with contextual factor adjustments — all updated on every pitch.

Base Layer

Empirical Win Expectancy

A lookup table derived from 140,824 historical game states sourced from Retrosheet play-by-play data (1903–2025), with era-weighted recency adjustments favoring post-2014 Statcast-era baseball. For any combination of inning, half, outs, base state, score differential, and ball-strike count, the table returns an empirical home win frequency. Independently validated at 1.12% average absolute error against 5,080 historical states.

Adjustment Layer

15-Factor Logit Model

Fifteen contextual adjustment factors applied additively in logit space, shifting the baseline probability based on matchup-specific information the raw historical average doesn't capture. Factors include starting pitcher quality (FIP/xFIP), bullpen availability, pitcher fatigue, park factors, platoon splits, team season quality via Log5, batter quality (wRC+), defensive metrics (OAA), and three Statcast in-game signals. Factor weights cross-validated against live market pricing.

Pregame Prior

Pinnacle Bayesian Anchor

Before the first pitch, the model is 100% anchored to devigged Pinnacle closing moneylines. As game evidence accumulates, the prior decays via a logistic schedule modulated by outs elapsed and absolute score differential. By the 5th inning of a tied game, the prior retains roughly 33% influence. In a blowout, it decays to under 20% by inning 5.

Edge Case

Ghost Runner Model

Dedicated empirical probability tables for post-2020 Manfred runner rule states, built from 917 decided extra-inning games. Key finding: the market systematically overprices home teams at 53–57% when the empirical home win rate since the rule change is 49.4%. Captures specific states like bottom-of-extras down 1 run (43.1% vs. 36.5% conventional estimate).

Why MLB

Baseball is the most quantifiable major professional sport for in-game prediction modeling. Every plate appearance is a discrete, independent event with rich observable covariates — pitch velocity, spin rate, exit velocity, launch angle. A 162-game regular season with roughly 70 plate appearances per game produces approximately 185,000 independent modeling events per team per year.

Unlike basketball or football, where continuous play creates temporal dependencies that degrade state-space models, baseball's discrete sequential structure maps directly to Markov chain transition matrices — the mathematical framework the base model uses. Historical data extends back over a century via Retrosheet, and the Statcast era (2015–present) provides granular biomechanical measurements on every pitch.

Approach

The Research
Methodology

Six non-negotiable principles enforced by the system as architectural constraints — not left to researcher discretion.

01
Statistical Integrity

Every factor must survive out-of-sample validation before it earns a nonzero weight. Walk-forward validation across full MLB seasons is the minimum standard. Our factor ablation process explicitly tests every active factor's marginal contribution — three candidates were zeroed after testing showed they degraded accuracy.

02
Domain Causality

Every signal must have a causal mechanism in baseball physics or game theory. A pitcher's fastball velocity declining 1.5 mph predicts degradation because biomechanical fatigue reduces spin efficiency. We do not trade on patterns that lack causal explanation — even if they backtest well.

03
Execution Realism

Backtests use actual order book data — real bid/ask spreads, real depth at each price level, real fee schedules. We model fill rates, queue priority, and the reality that spreads widen after scoring events. A strategy that can't execute in a live order book is not a strategy.

04
Honest Attribution

Every prediction is logged with a timestamp, the full game state, all factor deltas, and the final probability output. All model outputs are benchmarked against closing prices to compute closing line value — the gold standard for measuring genuine edge.

05
Layered Risk Controls

Position sizing follows fractional Kelly criterion with a meaningful implied edge threshold. The system enforces per-ticker limits, session loss gates, forced exits via IOC orders, and a three-tier stop-loss system. No single game, no single night, can create catastrophic loss.

06
Publish-Ready Standards

Every component is documented to academic standards. Theoretical foundations cite peer-reviewed literature on Bayesian sports modeling, Markov chain baseball theory, and prediction market microstructure. Our research library includes 15+ papers supporting the model architecture.
